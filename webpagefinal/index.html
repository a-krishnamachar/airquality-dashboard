AQI Meaning

Air Quality is measured most commonly today by a reading of "PM2.5". This is 'fine particulate matter', tiny particles that are a concern when elevated. It refers (name gives it away a bit) to particles that are ≤2.5 microns in width. For reference, this is only a fraction of the width of a human hair (1/30th!).

The EPA's current standard for 'healthy' air is as follows.
Short-term (24-hr): 35 µg/m^3
Long-term (1 yr): 12µg/m^3

Keep this in mind when browsing some of the more affected sections of the world.
